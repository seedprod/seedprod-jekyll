I have discovered that lots of people are not securing their wp-content or uploads directory from Apache Indexes, therefore browsable. In the process I also discovered plugins such as BackupBuddy and maybe other ones in the WP Repo are storing copies of the site in the wp-content/uploads folder.

For example if I do a google search for "/wp-content/uploads/backupbuddy_backups/" in the case of BackupBuddy I'm presented with all the listings where I can access this folder. Some people use the option to store locally instead of a remote destination. I can then download an entire zip of the site including the database credentials.

I have notified BackupBuddy and WordPress.org.

If you have BackupBuddy installed you need to 1 of the 2 solutions listed below:

  1. Disable Apache Indexes.
    <Directory /www/somefolder>
        Options -Indexes FollowSymLinks
        AllowOverride None
    </Directory>

  2. Add a blank index.php to the wp-content/uploads folder and the wp-content/backupbuddy_backups and the wp-content/backupbuddy_temp folder.

    3. I'm in the process of checking other backup plugins in the WordPress Repo.

    To test if your site is vulnerable just goto http://yoursite.com/wp-content/uploads/ If you see sensitive information you need to do one of the steps above.
